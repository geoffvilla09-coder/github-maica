Pinakbet Recipe

Description: Pinakbet (or Pakbet) is a nutritious Filipino vegetable stew from the Ilocos region, featuring a medley of local veggies like eggplant, okra, and bitter melon, simmered with pork and fermented shrimp paste (bagoong) for a savory, slightly salty flavor. It's healthy, hearty, and best served with rice. Serves 4-6. Prep time: 15 mins. Cook time: 30 mins.

Ingredients

  • 300g (0.66 lbs) pork belly or shrimp, cut into small pieces (optional for vegetarian version, omit and use more bagoong)
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons bagoong isda (fermented fish sauce) or bagoong alamang (shrimp paste)
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • 1 medium eggplant (talong), sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 10-12 pieces okra (lady's fingers), trimmed and halved
  • 1 bitter melon (ampalaya), seeded and sliced into thin half-moons
  • 1 small pumpkin or squash (kalabasa), peeled and cubed
  • 10-12 string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste (use sparingly due to bagoong)
  • 1 long green chili (siling haba), optional for mild heat

Instructions

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large wok or pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger (if using) until fragrant and softened, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the pork pieces (if using) and cook until lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Stir in the bagoong and cook for another 2 minutes to release its salty, umami flavors.
  3. Pour in the water or broth and bring to a boil. Add the tomatoes and long green chili (if using). Simmer for 3-4 minutes until tomatoes soften.
  4. Add the harder vegetables first: pumpkin or squash cubes and string beans. Cover and simmer for 5-7 minutes until they begin to soften.
  5. Stir in the eggplant, okra, and bitter melon. Simmer uncovered for another 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all vegetables are tender but not overcooked (they should retain some crunch).
  6. Taste the broth and adjust with a pinch of salt or pepper if needed (bagoong is usually salty enough). If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of water.
  7. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 2-3 minutes to allow flavors to meld. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tip: Soak the bitter melon slices in salted water for 10 minutes before cooking to reduce bitterness. For a vegan version, skip the pork and use vegetable broth with extra bagoong. Fresh veggies make the best Pinakbet—adjust quantities based on availability!
